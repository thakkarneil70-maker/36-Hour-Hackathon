      A parabola is the set of points equidistant from a focus (point) and directrix (line).

      Property: PF = PD
      Distance to Focus = Distance to Directrix

      Derivation: PF = PD

      Why every point on a parabola is equidistant from the focus and directrix

      1

      Definition of a Parabola

      A parabola is defined as the locus of all points that are equidistant from a fixed point (the Focus F) and a fixed line (the Directrix).

      2

      Setting Up Coordinates

      Place the vertex at the origin. Let the focus be at F(0, a) and the directrix be the line y = −a, where a is the focal length.

      Focus: F(0, a)  |  Directrix: y = −a
      3

      Distance from P(x, y) to Focus

      Using the distance formula:

      PF = √(x² + (y − a)²)
      4

      Distance from P(x, y) to Directrix

      The perpendicular distance from point P to the line y = −a is:

      PD = |y − (−a)| = |y + a|
      5

      Setting PF = PD

      By the definition of a parabola:

      √(x² + (y − a)²) = |y + a|

      Squaring both sides:

      x² + (y − a)² = (y + a)²
      6

      Expanding & Simplifying

      x² + y² − 2ay + a² = y² + 2ay + a²

      Cancel y² and a² from both sides:

      x² − 2ay = 2ay
      x² = 4ay
      ✓

      Conclusion

      The equation x² = 4ay is the standard equation of a parabola.

      🎯

      Since we derived this equation by setting PF = PD, this proves that for every single point on the parabola, the distance to the focus equals the distance to the directrix.

      PF = PD   ∀ P ∈ Parabola
